Engineering Design & Innovation @ VIT

Course Objectives

  • Foster critical thinking & problem solving through real-world challenges.
  • Evaluate and justify tools, methods & design decisions.
  • Drive long-term, interdisciplinary & student-centric learning.
  • Provide authentic, team-based experiences to build professionalism.
  • Cultivate entrepreneurship and a research-driven mindset.

About EDI @ VIT

The EDI program (CS2242 & CS3060) empowers students to solve socially relevant problems by applying engineering principles, interdisciplinary collaboration, and design thinking. Over 8 lab hours/week, teams prototype solutions, receive mentor guidance, and showcase impact-driven innovations.

  • Hands-on exposure to industry-standard tools & processes.
  • Emphasis on creativity, rapid prototyping & iteration.
  • Mentorship model: faculty act as facilitators, not lecturers.

Project-Centric Learning (PCL)

PCL places students at the center of inquiry, guiding them to define open-ended questions and engineer solutions. Domains include Healthcare, Agriculture, Smart Cities, Energy & Social Welfare (e.g., Swaccha Bharat).

  • Active, inquiry-based pedagogy over rote memorization.
  • Interdisciplinary challenges bridge multiple engineering fields.
  • Literature surveys and evidence-based design prevent reinvention.
  • Bloom’s Taxonomy in action: analyze, design, apply & evaluate.

Sample EDI Projects

  • IoT wearable for elderly health monitoring
  • AI-driven soil moisture & irrigation system
  • Autonomous drone for campus safety patrols
  • Smart waste bin for automated segregation

Groups of 4–5 students select a problem, perform needs analysis, prototype solutions, and validate results. Evaluation criteria include technical feasibility, creativity, user impact and teamwork effectiveness.

Roles in PCL

Faculty

  • Facilitate rather than dictate; mentor teams.
  • Guide time management & technical problem-solving.
  • Encourage metacognition, critical analysis & reflection.

Students

  • Own learning: ideate, research & prototype independently.
  • Collaborate effectively; navigate group dynamics.
  • Develop inquiry skills: model, justify & evaluate solutions.
